The Abstract Attack Chain

Before diving into specific tactics, you must understand our underlying methodology. We rarely rely on "hacking the platform" directly, as bypassing modern encryption is mathematically unfeasible. Instead, we follow a reliable, repeatable series of steps designed to exploit the human operating the application.

Our objective is always to trick the platform's authentication servers into believing that we are you. We achieve this through the following lifecycle:

  1. Target Selection & Reconnaissance: We identify a valuable Telegram account (e.g., a high-subscriber channel admin, a crypto trader, or a user with a premium/rare username). We gather their phone number or existing Telegram aliases to prepare the login prompt.
  2. The Lure (Delivery): We craft a highly specific, deceptive scenario—via a direct message, a malicious file payload, or an urgent email—designed to bypass the target's critical thinking.
  3. Interception: Telegram will send a 5-digit login code to the target's app or via SMS. Our entire attack infrastructure is designed specifically to capture this single string of numbers.
  4. Execution: We input the intercepted code into our own Telegram client, successfully logging into the target's account.
  5. Persistence & Lockdown: Immediately upon entry, we navigate to the active sessions menu and forcefully kick out the original user's devices. We then change the recovery email and set our own Two-Step Verification password, locking the true owner out permanently.
01

Malware & OTP Interception

Telegram accounts are intrinsically tied to phone numbers, but the application itself handles the authentication codes (OTPs) natively. If we can compromise the underlying operating system of your device—whether it's a PC or a smartphone—we completely bypass the need for human interaction. The malware simply reads the code for us as it arrives.

Tool Categories Deployed

  • Infostealers: Specialized malware engineered to silently scrape browser caches, copy active session tokens, and export credential databases to remote servers.
  • Remote Access Trojans (RATs): Covert software that provides us with real-time, administrative control over a victim's device, allowing us to view their screen and intercept notifications natively.
  • SMS Interceptors / Mobile Spyware: Mobile payloads disguised as legitimate apps that silently forward all incoming SMS messages (including 2FA codes) to our infrastructure.

Example Tactic: The Fake Desktop Update

We host a sophisticated website that mirrors the official Telegram desktop download page. By utilizing SEO manipulation or purchasing malicious ad placements, we ensure our site appears at the top of search engine results.

When a user downloads what they believe to be the "Telegram Desktop Installer," they actually execute a customized infostealer. We trigger a login attempt from our remote server, prompting Telegram to send the 5-digit code to the victim's open Telegram app. Our malware instantly reads the incoming notification, transmits the code back to our command server, and we authenticate—often before the victim even glances at their screen.

02

Physical Access & Session Hijacking

The quietest and most devastating way into a Telegram account requires zero coding skills. Telegram's seamless multi-device synchronization is a feature we heavily exploit. Security protocols often assume that the person holding an unlocked device is the authorized user. If we have physical access to a target's unlocked phone for mere seconds, the account is compromised.

Tool Categories Deployed

  • Keystroke Injection Devices (BadUSBs): Hardware disguised as normal USB flash drives that act as a keyboard. When plugged into an unlocked computer, they can execute a programmed sequence of keypresses in milliseconds to link a session or steal a local session database.
  • Stalkerware: Commercially available surveillance applications installed directly onto an unattended, unlocked mobile device.
  • Native Application Exploitation: Simply weaponizing Telegram's built-in "Linked Devices" QR scanner against the user without the need for external software.

Example Tactic: The Ghost Session

We observe a target at a cafe, co-working space, or office. The target leaves their phone unlocked on the table to grab a coffee or speak with a colleague.

In less than 10 seconds, we open their Telegram app, navigate to Settings > Devices > Link Desktop Device. We scan a QR code displayed on our own tablet or laptop nearby. We have just created an active, fully authorized "Telegram Web" session on our machine. We place the target's phone exactly back where it was. They return, entirely unaware that we now have complete, real-time access to all their chats, contacts, and media.

03

Social Engineering & Trust Exploitation

Why spend days developing custom malware to steal a code when you can simply ask the target to hand it to you? Social engineering is the art of manipulating human psychology. We don't attack the encryption; we exploit your trust, panic, or desire to help.

Tool Categories Deployed

  • OSINT (Open Source Intelligence) Frameworks: Aggregation tools used to legally scrape a target's background, social circles, and workplace structure to build a highly convincing pretext.
  • Voice Cloning AI: Deepfake audio tools utilized to clone the voice of a target's friend or boss from public videos, allowing us to send authentic-sounding voice notes requesting account assistance.
  • Caller ID Spoofing: VoIP services configured to make our phone call appear as if it is originating from "Telegram Support" or a trusted contact's phone number.

Example Tactic: The "Accidental Code" Scam

We successfully compromise User A's account. We immediately review their chat history to identify close friends or family. We message User B (a close friend) from User A's account.

"Hey! I'm trying to log into my new phone but my SMS isn't working. I told Telegram to send the recovery code to my trusted emergency contact (which is you). Can you copy the 5-digit code you just got from Telegram so I can log in?"

Simultaneously, we trigger a legitimate login attempt using User B's phone number. User B receives an official Telegram code. Thinking they are helping their friend out of a frustrating tech issue, User B copies the code and sends it to us in the chat. We instantly use that code to steal User B's account, and the cycle continues.

04

Phishing Portals

Phishing scales beautifully. It is about creating a perfect, pixel-accurate illusion. We direct targets to a webpage or a Telegram bot that we control, designed to perfectly mimic Telegram's official Web interface or support channels. The objective is to induce a state of panic or greed, forcing the user to willingly type in their phone number and the subsequent login code.

Tool Categories Deployed

  • Adversary-in-the-Middle (AitM) Proxies: Advanced phishing frameworks that sit directly between the target and the real Telegram servers, seamlessly relaying the 5-digit OTPs and capturing the resulting session cookies in real-time to bypass normal 2FA.
  • Typo-squatting Generators: Automated scripts that identify and register visually identical domain names (e.g., using Cyrillic characters to mimic English letters in URLs).
  • Bulletproof Hosting: Specialized infrastructure providers located in jurisdictions that intentionally ignore DMCA or legal takedown requests, keeping our fake login portals online longer.

Example Tactic: The "Account Deletion" Bot

We create a Telegram bot named "Telegram Security Notifications" featuring the official logo. We send mass, automated messages to thousands of users:

"URGENT: Your account has been flagged for violating our Terms of Service regarding spam. Your account will be permanently deleted in 12 hours. To appeal this decision, click here to verify your identity."

The panicked user clicks the link, arriving at our counterfeit verification portal. The site demands their phone number. When entered, our backend server requests a real login code from Telegram. Our fake site then prompts the user: "Enter the verification code sent to your app to cancel the deletion." The user types it in out of fear, effectively handing us the keys to their digital life.

The Defenses That Stop Us

We rely on targets taking the path of least resistance. When a user implements these specific Telegram security features, our automated tools fail entirely, and our manual attacks become vastly more time-consuming. When we encounter a hardened account, we almost always abandon the attack and move on to an easier target.

  • Two-Step Verification (Cloud Password): This is the ultimate, non-negotiable roadblock. Set a strong password. Even if we successfully steal your SMS or app OTP code through phishing or malware, we absolutely cannot log in without this secondary, alphanumeric password.
  • Auditing Active Sessions: Targets who routinely check Settings > Devices and terminate unrecognized or old sessions effectively neutralize our physical access and "Ghost Session" attacks.
  • Disabling Lock-Screen Previews: Prevent physical attackers from reading your codes by configuring your phone's OS to hide the content of notifications until the device is biometrically unlocked.
  • Code Literacy & Skepticism: Telegram explicitly states in every login message: "Do not give this code to anyone, even if they say they are from Telegram." Official support will never, ever ask for your login code.
  • Restricting Privacy Settings: Restricting "Who can find me by my phone number" and "Who can add me to groups/channels" to "My Contacts" severely limits our ability to perform reconnaissance.
